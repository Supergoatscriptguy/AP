Earth

Our home

Overview

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only object in the Universe known to host life.

The Earth's surface is 71% covered with water, with the remainder consisting of continents and islands.

  • Distance from Sun: 93 million miles (1 AU)
  • Diameter: 7,918 miles
  • Length of Day: 24 hours
  • Length of Year: 365.25 days
  • Surface Temperature: -88°F to 136°F

Earth seen from space, showing the oceans, clouds, and continents.

Key Features

Life-Supporting Atmosphere

Earth's rare atmosphere consists of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and small amounts of other gases. The ozone layer in the stratosphere protects humans, animals, and plants from harmful ultraviolet radiation from the Sun.

Magnetic Field

Earth's iron core generates a magnetic field that protects the Earth from solar radiation and cosmic rays, allowing life.

Dynamic Surface

Plate tectonics slowly rearange Earth's surface, which is why we have mountains, valleys, and other features.

Did You Know?

  • Earth is the densest planet in our solar system.
  • It's the only planet not named after a Greek or Roman god.
  • Our Moon, is slowly moving away from us at about 3.8 cm per year.
  • Earth compared to the Moon is HUGE.
