Stars

Stars are huge balls of plasma held together by gravity.

The Sun

Our star and the center of our solar system. The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star that contains 99.86% of the Solar System's mass and provides the energy that sustains life on Earth.

sirius-a

The brightest star in Earth's night sky, also known as the Dog Star. sirius-a is actually a binary star system consisting of a bright main-sequence star (sirius-a A) and a faint white dwarf companion (sirius-a B).

View More Info

Betelgeuse

A red supergiant star in the constellation Orion, approximately 700 times larger than the Sun. Betelgeuse is nearing the end of its life and is expected to explode as a supernova within the next 100,000 years.
